Microsoft Band

My first smartwatch. Discontinued. Perhaps for the better.

Microsoft Band was a hardware that I enjoyed using, but also had clear fallacies. When I heard that they are going to discontinue, it was evident that the branding didn't catch on. However, I also realized that I stopped wearing it as soon as I got to Microsoft. Perhaps it was because of the lack of flexibility in the rubber materia they used. The hook/joiner part of the watch was also very clunky, making it harder for users to be used to having it worn at all times. Howver, I do want to recognize that Microsoft Band offered services and features that truly joined my phone to the watch.

Fitbit

Current choice of hardware. Keeps track of steps, calories, heartrate, etc and sends it to my phone. Currently dominating the smartwatch market.

I'm not a big fan of smartwatches. I had the opportunity to try one and keep one from the summit I went to. Maybe the same issue is happening with the band; I hate having things on my wrist due to the activities I do (violin, typing, etc). However, I did notice that while it does cover less features, it felt more natural like a watch on people. I believe that the challenge that this market faces is how to make it more natural.

Cloud Gate

Chicago, IL

Being in Chicago, one place you can not *not* visit is the Cloud Gate, more famously known as "the Bean." It was interesting to me that despite its original name, Cloud Gate, many people refer to this structure as the bean, which made me wonder the relevance of the look of an interface to its actual name. It was indeed interesting to walk under and through the bean (and I could see how the artist intended it to be like a cloud gate from the reflection of myself on the structure), but even to me, "the bean" sounds more welcoming and funny.

Perkins Entrance

Durham, NC

This door really confuses me; why is the sensor so far from the actual doors? I found this to be a great example of bad interface, especially if the trigger of the interface is time-sensitive. I had times where I would swipe, but did not make it in time to open the unlocked doors. Few possibilities lie here: 1) put it between the two entrances. 2) make the duration longer. 3) keep the doors unlocked during operating hours.

Modern Music Score

Durham, NC

As modern composers start to explore different ways to notate music, graphical notation is something that composers started to explore as an option. This piece, Poeme Eletronique by Edgar Varese is a music without a score. This was my attempt to express Varese's music on paper. This also comes with a page just full of what sound each graphical figure represents.
